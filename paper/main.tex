\title{Learned Nyquist: Fundamental Aliasing Limits of\\ Implicit Neural Representations}

\begin{abstract}
Implicit neural representations (INRs)---SIREN and Fourier-feature coordinate networks---are
increasingly used as continuous models of signals. A recent \emph{achievability} sampling theory
addresses signals that are themselves INRs; we address the \emph{converse} question that governs
practice, where the signal is arbitrary and observed at finite, noisy, nonuniform samples. A
fixed-feature INR (fixed feature frequencies, linear output layer) represents only a
\emph{structured} frequency set $\Lam$. Casting its least-squares fit as generalized sampling on
this dictionary, we prove: (i) achievability---if the signal spectrum lies in $\Lam$ and the
samples render the dictionary matrix full column rank, a bandwidth-matched INR recovers it with
closed-form noise gain $\kappa=1/\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)$; (ii) a converse---out-of-band energy is
\emph{folded} onto $\Lam$ with an error floor no fixed-feature INR can beat, and for
grid-coherent tones the sample residual is \emph{exactly zero} while the reconstruction error is
at least the full out-of-band energy (\emph{silent aliasing}), with a \emph{non-classical} fold
target when $\Lam$ is structured; (iii) an exact decomposition of the estimator's average error
into an estimation-variance term and an aliasing-variance term that no sampling density uniformly
removes. We give a bandwidth-matched estimator and a ground-truth-free diagnostic with a measured
operating characteristic (AUC $1.0$ off the provably-undetectable coherent worst case), validate
every identity against Monte-Carlo, and demonstrate both sides of the limit on real audio and
scientific signals---the phenomenon persisting, fold-for-fold, for trained \emph{nonlinear} and
2-D INRs. The classical uniform-lowpass fold is one special case; the structured-set fold law,
the exact-fold characterization of silent aliasing, and the diagnostic's operating characteristic
are new.
\end{abstract}

\begin{keywords}
Implicit neural representations, sampling theory, aliasing, generalized sampling, coordinate networks.
\end{keywords}

\section{Introduction}
Implicit neural representations parameterize a signal as a coordinate network
$f_\theta:\R^d\!\to\!\R$ and are now a default continuous model for audio, images, volumes, and
scientific fields \cite{sitzmann2020siren,tancik2020fourier,mildenhall2020nerf}. A large literature
engineers their \emph{architectures} against spectral bias and aliasing---periodic activations,
Fourier features, band-limited and anti-aliased variants
\cite{rahaman2019spectral,shabanov2024banf,lindell2022bacon,barron2021mipnerf,novello2025tuner,alsakabi2025fmsiren}---and
a recent thread gives a \emph{sampling theory}: Najaf and Ongie \cite{najaf2024sampling} and the
super-resolution follow-up \cite{najaf2025superres} prove that a signal which \emph{is} a shallow
INR is exactly recoverable from enough noiseless Fourier measurements, and sinc-activated INRs
admit Shannon-style reconstruction guarantees \cite{saratchandran2024activations}. These are
\emph{achievability} statements about realizable signals \cite{jayasundara2026survey}.

\emph{The gap.} In practice the signal is not an INR: it is an arbitrary waveform observed at
finitely many, often nonuniform, noisy samples. The achievability theory is noiseless and
realizable-signal by construction (misspecification appears in \cite{najaf2024sampling} only as an
experiment); nothing above says when the task is possible, how the answer depends on the network's
capacity, or what failure looks like. We supply the converse. The key
object is the INR's \emph{representable frequency set} $\Lam$: for a Fourier-feature/periodic
coordinate network the output is a combination of complex exponentials whose frequencies are integer
combinations of the base feature frequencies---a set that grows with width and depth
\cite{yuce2022structured,novello2025tuner}. A signal is representable only insofar as its spectrum
lies in $\Lam$.

\emph{Scope and contributions.} Our theorems concern the analytically exact case: a fixed-feature
INR whose output layer is linear over $\Lam$. Its least-squares fit is generalized (nonharmonic)
sampling on the finite dictionary $\Lam$ \cite{adcock2016generalized,unser2000sampling}; that recast
is the mechanism. On top of it we establish the INR-specific facts: (1) an achievability bound with
a closed-form noise gain (Thm.~\ref{thm:ach}); (2) a converse in which out-of-band energy folds onto
$\Lam$ with an error floor, and an exact-fold characterization of \emph{silent aliasing}---zero
sample residual with full-energy reconstruction failure---whose target, for structured $\Lam$, is
unlike the classical lowpass law (Thm.~\ref{thm:conv}); (3) an exact average-case error
decomposition separating estimation variance from an aliasing-variance term that no sampling
density uniformly removes (Thm.~\ref{thm:stat}); and (4) a bandwidth-matched estimator, a
ground-truth-free diagnostic with a measured ROC, Monte-Carlo-validated theory, and real-data
demonstrations of both sides, which persist---fold-for-fold---for trained nonlinear and 2-D INRs.

\emph{Relation to prior analyses.} When $\Lam=\{-B,\dots,B\}$ and sampling is uniform, our converse
reduces to classical aliasing (Rem.~\ref{rem:classical}). Three adjacent lines must be
distinguished. \emph{Basis mismatch} \cite{chi2011sensitivity} analyzes the same least-squares bias
for a tone \emph{near} a dictionary atom; our silent-aliasing regime is the complementary
\emph{far} regime, where a grid-coherent tone arbitrarily far from $\Lam$ folds with zero residual
(Thm.~\ref{thm:conv}(iii)). The \emph{spectral window} of nonuniform sampling
\cite{vanderplas2018lomb} predicts where out-of-band power appears in a periodogram; our fold
target is its dictionary-projection analogue, and we pair it with achievability, noise, and a
diagnostic. A \emph{generalized aliasing decomposition} for least-squares/random-feature models
\cite{transtrum2024gad} and an NTK-based lower bound from \emph{missing} frequencies
\cite{ma2025robustifying} both bound misspecification error; neither gives the structured fold
law (where the energy \emph{lands}), the exact-fold silent regime, or the detection question that
we address. Empirically, trained SIRENs have been observed to plant off-grid aliases
\cite{chandravamsi2025spectral}; we supply the theory and the diagnostic for that observation.

\section{Signal model and representable band}
Let $f:[0,1)\!\to\!\R$ be observed at samples $t_1,\dots,t_N$ (possibly nonuniform) as
$y_j=f(t_j)+\varepsilon_j$. A fixed-feature INR with feature set inducing
$\Lam=\{\omega_1,\dots,\omega_m\}\subset\R$ has model class
$\Mcal_\Lam=\{\sum_k c_k e_{\omega_k}\}$ where $e_\omega(x)=e^{i2\pi\omega x}$; deeper/periodic
networks enlarge $\Lam$ to integer combinations of base frequencies \cite{yuce2022structured}. Define
the \emph{synthesis matrix} $\Phimat\in\C^{N\times m}$, $\Phimat_{jk}=e_{\omega_k}(t_j)$; fitting the
INR is the least-squares problem $\hat{\bm c}=\arg\min_{\bm c}\norm{\bm y-\Phimat\bm c}^2$ (the
released code optionally adds a negligible ridge, $10^{-8}$--$10^{-6}$ in the experiments).
Write $f=f_{\mathrm{in}}+f_{\mathrm{out}}$ where $f_{\mathrm{in}}=\sum_k c^\star_k e_{\omega_k}
\in\Mcal_\Lam$ and $f_{\mathrm{out}}=\sum_l a_l e_{\nu_l}$ is a finite exponential sum on
frequencies $\nu_l\notin\Lam$ (synthesis matrix $\Phimat_{\mathrm{out}}$), so
$\bm y=\Phimat\bm c^\star+\bm g+\bm\varepsilon$ with
$g_j=f_{\mathrm{out}}(t_j)$. The lower \emph{frame bound} is $A=\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)^2$; $A>0$ iff
$\Phimat$ has full column rank ($m\le N$, generic positions), i.e.\ the samples determine
$\Mcal_\Lam$.

\section{Fundamental limits}
\begin{theorem}[Achievability]\label{thm:ach}
If $f_{\mathrm{out}}=0$ and $\Phimat$ has full column rank ($\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)>0$), then
$\norm{\hat{\bm c}-\bm c^\star}\le\kappa\,\norm{\bm\varepsilon}$ with noise gain
$\kappa=\norm{(\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)^{-1}\Phimat^{*}}_2=1/\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)$, and for white noise
of variance $\sigma^2$, $\mathbb E\norm{\hat{\bm c}-\bm c^\star}^2=\sigma^2\tr((\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)^{-1})$.
\end{theorem}
Full column rank needs $N\ge m=|\Lam|$; for the special case $\Lam=\{-B,\dots,B\}$ this is
consistent with the Landau density $N\gtrsim 2B$ \cite{landau1967necessary}, but for a sparse
structured $\Lam$ the binding requirement is $N\ge|\Lam|$, not the band extent. Rank alone prices
nothing: $\kappa$ can be arbitrarily large for clustered samples---the bound is achievability at
the price $\kappa$, which generalized sampling controls via density conditions
\cite{adcock2016generalized}.

\begin{theorem}[Converse and aliasing]\label{thm:conv}
Assume $\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)>0$ and let $P=\Phimat(\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)^{-1}\Phimat^{*}$,
$\bm M=(\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)^{-1}\Phimat^{*}$. With $f_{\mathrm{out}}\neq0$ the estimate is
biased, $\hat{\bm c}=\bm c^\star+\Delta\bm c$, $\Delta\bm c=\bm M\bm g$, and:
(i) no INR in $\Mcal_\Lam$ fits the noiseless samples better than
$\min_{\bm c}\norm{\bm y-\Phimat\bm c}=\norm{(\bm I-P)\bm g}$;
(ii) for a tone $a\,e_\nu$ with sample vector $\bm\phi_\nu$, the residual is
$|a|\,\norm{\bm\phi_\nu}\sin\theta_\nu$ ($\theta_\nu$ the principal angle between $\bm\phi_\nu$
and $\mathrm{range}\,\Phimat$) while the spectral bias is $\norm{\Delta\bm c}=|a|\norm{\bm M\bm\phi_\nu}$;
(iii) (\emph{silent aliasing}) if $\Lam\subset\mathbb Z$, the samples lie on a rate-$Q$ grid
($t_j\in\{0,\dots,Q{-}1\}/Q$), and $\nu\in\mathbb Z\setminus\Lam$ satisfies
$\nu\equiv\omega_k\ (\mathrm{mod}\ Q)$, then $\bm\phi_\nu=\bm\phi_{\omega_k}$ exactly: the residual
is \emph{zero} while $\norm{\Delta\bm c}=|a|$, and every $h\in\Mcal_\Lam$ has
$\norm{h-f}_{L^2[0,1)}\ge\norm{f_{\mathrm{out}}}_{L^2}$---full-energy reconstruction failure,
invisible in-sample, at any $N$ and with $\nu$ arbitrarily far from $\Lam$.
\end{theorem}
We call such a fold \emph{coherent} with the sampling; in general, coherence means
$\norm{\bm M\bm\phi_\nu}$ stays bounded away from zero as the density grows. The \emph{fold
target} is defined as $\omega_{k^\star}$, $k^\star=\arg\max_k|\Delta c_k|$: for generic nonuniform
samples it is the dictionary analogue of the peak of the sampled spectral window familiar from
Lomb--Scargle analysis \cite{vanderplas2018lomb}. The near-collision regime
$\nu\to\omega_k$ (where the residual also vanishes but so does the reconstruction error) is the
basis-mismatch sensitivity of \cite{chi2011sensitivity}, governed by super-resolution conditioning
\cite{candes2014superres}; (iii) is its harmful far counterpart.

\begin{remark}\label{rem:classical}
For uniform $t_j=j/N$ and $\Lam$ an integer lowpass band, the fold target is the unique element of
$\Lam$ \emph{congruent to} $\nu \bmod N$ (and $\Delta\bm c=\bm 0$ if the residue class misses
$\Lam$): classical aliasing \cite{shannon1949} is the coherent special case $Q=N$. For structured $\Lam$ and
grid-subset or nonuniform samples the fold target is non-classical (Fig.~\ref{fig:silent}).
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Average-case decomposition]\label{thm:stat}
Assume $\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)>0$, and let the out-of-band coefficients $a_l$ be zero-mean,
uncorrelated, with $\mathbb E|a_l|^2=p_l$,
independent of the noise, and $\mathbb E[\bm\varepsilon\bm\varepsilon^{*}]=\sigma^2\bm I$. Then the
mean-squared error of the least-squares estimator is exactly
\begin{equation}\label{eq:decomp}
\mathbb E\norm{\hat{\bm c}-\bm c^\star}^2
=\underbrace{\sigma^2\tr\!\big((\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)^{-1}\big)}_{\text{estimation variance}}
+\underbrace{\tr\!\big(\bm M\bm\Sigma_g\bm M^{*}\big)}_{\text{aliasing variance}},
\end{equation}
with $\bm\Sigma_g=\Phimat_{\mathrm{out}}\,\mathrm{diag}(\bm p)\,\Phimat_{\mathrm{out}}^{*}$.
\end{theorem}
Equation \eqref{eq:decomp} is a fixed-$N$ identity. The estimation term decays like $\sigma^2m/N$
for well-conditioned sampling, but the aliasing term is not \emph{uniformly} removable: at every
density some tone folds coherently and keeps it at full power
($\norm{\bm M\bm\phi_\nu}=1$ exactly for grid sampling, Thm.~\ref{thm:conv}(iii); in the
near-collision limit otherwise). For a \emph{fixed} tone the term converges to its window-leakage energy
onto $\Mcal_\Lam$: exactly zero for integer-disjoint tones under uniform sampling ($O(m/N)$ in
expectation under i.i.d.\ sampling), but the positive constant $\sum_k\mathrm{sinc}^2(\nu-\omega_k)$
for non-integer $\nu$. The proofs are elementary least-squares/projection identities; each
statement---including every asymptotic regime above---is asserted against Monte-Carlo in the
released test suite.

\section{Matched estimator and diagnostic}
\emph{Bandwidth matching.} Estimating the essential band from the samples via a nonuniform
periodogram and choosing $\Lam$ to cover it places the signal in the Thm.~\ref{thm:ach} regime; this
is the estimator we use. \emph{Diagnostic.} Because a fold is invisible in-sample
(Thm.~\ref{thm:conv}(ii)--(iii)), we test band-limitation to $\Lam$ by fitting an enlarged
dictionary $\Lam\cup\Lam_{\mathrm{ring}}$ ($\Lam_{\mathrm{ring}}$ a ring of candidate frequencies
outside $\Lam$) and measuring the recovered energy fraction on the ring. The test is valid only in
the overdetermined regime $N>|\Lam\cup\Lam_{\mathrm{ring}}|$ (an underdetermined ridge fit spreads
energy everywhere and flags every signal), and its threshold should be null-calibrated; Sec.~5
measures its full operating characteristic. By Thm.~\ref{thm:conv}(iii) an \emph{exactly} coherent
grid fold is information-theoretically undetectable from the samples---the diagnostic's measured
collapse there is the converse made visible, not a defect of the test.

\section{Experiments}
Every identity is validated against Monte-Carlo (frame bounds, $\kappa$, aliasing floor, exact and
near-collision fold regimes, all three Thm.~\ref{thm:stat} asymptotic regimes, and the joint
decomposition \eqref{eq:decomp}). Fig.~\ref{fig:silent} shows the structured silent-aliasing
regime of Thm.~\ref{thm:conv}(iii): the INR fits the samples at the noise floor yet the recovered
spectrum carries the full tone energy on the exact non-classical fold atom. Fig.~\ref{fig:phase}
shows the phase transition with the exact theory overlaid.

\emph{Diagnostic ROC.} Over $500$ draws per condition (structured $\Lam$ as in
Fig.~\ref{fig:silent}, ring $[-30,30]\setminus\Lam$, $N\in\{80,150\}$ jittered samples, tone
amplitudes $0.25$--$1\times$ the in-band RMS, on- and off-grid $\nu$), the ring diagnostic
separates perfectly (AUC $1.000$; TPR $1.000$ at the null-calibrated threshold with FPR $0.05$;
the fixed $0.1$ threshold has zero false positives but misses the weakest tones). On the exact
coherent grid fold of Fig.~\ref{fig:silent} it collapses to below-chance (AUC $0.20$)---the
provably undetectable worst case---and in the underdetermined regime it flags $100\%$ of null
signals, confirming the validity requirement of Sec.~4 (released \texttt{diagnostic\_roc}).

\emph{Real data, both sides} (Fig.~\ref{fig:real}; mean${}\pm{}$s.d.\ over $10$
subsample/noise draws). Sweeping the INR bandwidth $B_{\mathrm{INR}}$ on a real signal and reading
the reconstruction-error knee gives the empirical learned-Nyquist rate. Mauna
Loa $\mathrm{CO_2}$ (NOAA; $N{=}414$ of $M{=}820$, nonuniform), after linear detrending, is
bandlimited (annual cycle), giving a sharp knee at its spectral peak: a narrow INR aliases it, a
matched INR recovers it ($\mathrm{RMSE}\ 0.42\pm0.00\!\to\!0.17\pm0.03$). Broadband speech (Open
Speech Repository; $N{=}1400$ of $M{=}2000$) shows the converse side: no width approaches the
$0.03$ noise floor---narrow bands sit on a truncation-plus-aliasing floor ($\mathrm{RMSE}\ge1.0$)
and widths approaching the sample budget explode from the Thm.~\ref{thm:ach} noise gain
($\kappa\to\infty$ as $m\to N$; $\mathrm{RMSE}\ 682\pm386$ at $B{=}650$). Smoothed sunspots (SILSO;
the smoothing is a documented lowpass proxy) behave like $\mathrm{CO_2}$
($0.92\pm0.01\!\to\!0.41\pm0.18$); the raw series behaves broadband. The phenomenon persists,
fold-for-fold, for trained \emph{nonlinear} INRs: training identical Fourier-feature networks with
and without an out-of-hull tone and taking the difference spectrum isolates where the trained net
puts the tone's energy---it matches the Hermitian-pair linear bias pattern bin-for-bin (correlation
$0.99/0.91/0.63$ over three sampling draws; measured peak equal to the predicted fold target in
two, and to a near-tied atom of the predicted pattern in the third), while a bandwidth-covering
trained network recovers real $\mathrm{CO_2}$ slightly better than the linear estimator
($0.107$ vs.\ $0.143$, one fixed draw).
Because the Gaussian feature scale is a \emph{soft} band edge (features have tails and the MLP
generates harmonics), the trained-INR persistence claim rests on this out-of-hull-tone ablation,
not on a scale sweep. Finally, a 2-D INR on sparse image pixels (Fig.~\ref{fig:image}) reproduces
the limit: an over-scaled band fits the observed pixels to $51$~dB while the full field is
moir\'e-corrupted ($16$~dB).

\section{Conclusion}
Fixed-feature INRs alias, and provably so: they represent only a structured band $\Lam$, out-of-band
energy folds onto $\Lam$ with an error floor, and for coherent folds the corruption is total yet
\emph{exactly} invisible in the sample fit. The limit is sharp, matched by a bandwidth-selecting
estimator, detectable from data everywhere except the provably-undetectable coherent worst case,
and visible on real signals, persisting fold-for-fold for trained nonlinear and
2-D INRs. Extending the converse to adaptive-frequency $\Lam$ (where the network can move atoms onto
the signal) and to the exact training dynamics are the natural next steps.
