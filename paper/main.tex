\begin{abstract}
Fixed Fourier-feature coordinate models---the linear core of Fourier-feature implicit
neural representations---fit a signal by least squares on an exponential dictionary
$\Lam$.  We analyze, and then \emph{design against}, the aliasing of out-of-band content.
(i)~\textbf{Structured indistinguishability.}  A visibility/aliasability calculus shows
that \emph{arbitrary nonuniform subsets of a sampling grid inherit the grid's aliasing
equivalence classes}: an integer tone congruent mod $Q$ to a model frequency is
\emph{exactly} indistinguishable from it---zero training residual, full-energy failure,
and no estimator can avoid it.  Irregular-on-a-grid sampling does \emph{not} break exact
aliasing.  (ii)~\textbf{Randomization breaks it, at a rate.}  Off-grid jitter destroys the
fold with visibility equal to the jitter characteristic function, and i.i.d.\ sampling
gives a finite-$N$, dictionary-referenced concentration bound on worst-case aliasability.
(iii)~\textbf{Certified design.}  We show the anti-aliasing objective is a
$D_s$-optimal / null-steering criterion on sample times, give a fast surrogate whose joint
objective (an ablation shows) is necessary, and---the new tool---a computable
\emph{continuum certificate} that guarantees worst-case aliasability over an entire band
from the samples alone, which finite-candidate bounds and random designs do not provide.
The design cuts held-out worst-case aliasability from $\AGrand$ (random) to
$\AGheld{\pm}\AGheldCI$ and certifies $\AGcert$ band-wide vs.\ $\AGcertR$, in 1-D and 2-D.
All closed forms are Monte-Carlo validated; trained SIREN/MLP networks are an explicitly
labeled empirical extension with failures reported.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Coordinate networks with Fourier features are standard continuous signal
models~\cite{sitzmann2020siren,tancik2020fourier,mildenhall2020nerf}.  Their analytically
exact core---the object of every theorem here---is the \emph{fixed Fourier-feature
coordinate model}: a finite frequency set $\Lam=\{\omega_1,\dots,\omega_m\}\subset\R$ with
linear coefficients, fit by least squares.  Trained nonlinear networks enter only as an
\emph{empirical extension} (Sec.~\ref{sec:exp}); adaptive/learned frequencies are out of
scope---we make no claim for ``all INRs''.

A practitioner choosing \emph{when} and \emph{where} to sample faces three questions when
the signal carries energy outside $\Lam$.  \textbf{Q1}: when is that energy exactly
invisible in the samples, fitting perfectly yet corrupting the reconstruction?
\textbf{Q2}: how does the sampling pattern change this?  \textbf{Q3}: can one \emph{design}
the samples to prevent it, with a guarantee?  This paper answers all three and closes the
loop with a constructive, certified design.

\emph{Contributions.}
(1)~A visibility/aliasability calculus and its central corrective (Thm.~\ref{thm:t1}):
arbitrary nonuniform \emph{grid subsets} inherit the grid's aliasing equivalence classes,
so irregular sampling on a grid does not break exact aliasing---with an
estimator-independent two-point converse.
(2)~A finite-$N$, dictionary-referenced quantification of how off-grid randomization
breaks the fold: a jitter characteristic-function law and an i.i.d.\ concentration bound
(Thm.~\ref{thm:t3}).
(3)~A \emph{certified constructive design} (Sec.~\ref{sec:design}): we recognize the
anti-aliasing objective as a $D_s$-optimal / null-steering criterion on sample times, give
a fast joint surrogate that an ablation shows is necessary, and prove a computable
\emph{continuum aliasability certificate} that guarantees the worst case over an entire
out-of-band interval from the samples only.
(4)~An honest empirical program: real-data both sides, a trained-network extension with
its SIREN failure reported, and 2-D, all with confidence intervals and released failing
runs.

\emph{Relation to prior work.}  Classical modulo-$N$ aliasing~\cite{shannon1949} is the
full-grid special case of Thm.~\ref{thm:t1}, which covers arbitrary grid subsets; the
generalized-sampling recast~\cite{adcock2016generalized,unser2000sampling} underlies our
least-squares view.  That randomized timing suppresses aliasing is
classical---Balakrishnan's characteristic-function attenuation and alias-free random
sampling~\cite{balakrishnan1962jitter,shapiro1960alias,beutler1970alias}; those are
asymptotic spectral-replica statements, whereas Thm.~\ref{thm:t3} gives finite-$N$,
dictionary-referenced control (a column-norm of the generalized aliasing
operator~\cite{transtrum2024gad}), uniform over candidates.  \textbf{Our design principle
is not new and we do not claim it}: protecting a model block while nulling its coupling to
a specified set is exactly $D_s$-optimal (nuisance-parameter) experimental
design~\cite{silvey1980optimal,atkinson2007optimum} and LCMV/MVDR
null-steering~\cite{frost1972lcmv,vantrees2002optimum}.  Our contribution is the
identifiability analysis that says \emph{which} cross-terms to null and \emph{why grid
designs cannot}, together with the \emph{continuum certificate}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:design})---a sample-only guarantee over a whole band, which the
finite-candidate bound and random designs lack.  Basis mismatch~\cite{chi2011sensitivity}
is the complementary near-atom regime; the Lomb--Scargle
window~\cite{vanderplas2018lomb} predicts periodogram leakage.  The structured-dictionary
view of INRs~\cite{yuce2022structured}, INR achievability
theorems~\cite{najaf2024sampling,najaf2025superres,saratchandran2024activations}, an NTK
missing-frequency floor~\cite{ma2025robustifying}, anti-aliasing INR
architectures~\cite{lindell2022bacon,barron2021mipnerf,shabanov2024banf}, and empirical
SIREN aliasing~\cite{chandravamsi2025spectral} are adjacent; none gives the grid-inheritance
corrective, the finite-$N$ bound, or a certified design.

\section{Setup}\label{sec:setup}
Signals live on $[0,1)$; $e_\omega(x)=e^{i2\pi\omega x}$; $\Mcal_\Lam=\{\sum_kc_ke_{\omega_k}\}$
with $\Lam$ fixed.  Samples $T=\{t_j\}_{j=1}^N$ give $S_Tf=(f(t_j))_j$, the synthesis
matrix $\Phimat\in\C^{N\times m}$ ($\Phimat_{jk}=e_{\omega_k}(t_j)$), and $\bm y=S_Tf+\bm
\varepsilon$.  Write $f=f_{\mathrm{in}}+f_{\mathrm{out}}$, $f_{\mathrm{out}}=\sum_la_le_{\nu_l}$,
$\nu_l\notin\Lam$.  Ordinary least squares $\hat{\bm c}=\Phimat^\dagger\bm y$ is used
throughout.  For $\nu\in\R$, the \emph{sample atom} $\bm\phi_\nu=(e^{i2\pi\nu t_j})_j$
defines the \emph{visibility} and \emph{aliasability}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:va}
\vt(\nu)=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt N}\norm{(\bm I-\bm P_\Lam)\bm\phi_\nu}\in[0,1],\qquad
\at(\nu)=\norm{\Phimat^\dagger\bm\phi_\nu},
\end{equation}
$\bm P_\Lam$ the projector onto $\range\Phimat$: $\vt$ is the sample energy the model
cannot absorb, $\at$ the coefficient bias a unit tone induces.  Under white noise,
$\norm{\hat{\bm c}-\bm c^\star}\le\norm{\bm\varepsilon}/\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)$ (standard;
Lemma~S1).  Converting coefficient error to signal error uses the continuous Gram
$G_{L^2}[k,\ell]=e^{i\pi\delta}\operatorname{sinc}\delta$ ($\delta=\omega_\ell-\omega_k$)
with Riesz bounds, and $\norm{\hat f-f}_{L^2}^2$ splits exactly into a modeled-component
(aliasing-bias$+$noise) term, a cross term, and a truncation term $\bm a^{*}G_{\mathrm{out}}
\bm a$ that \emph{no} sampling removes (Prop.~S2; used in Fig.~\ref{fig:matrix}d and the
real-data results).

\section{Structured indistinguishability}\label{sec:t1}
\begin{theorem}[Exact equivalence on grid subsets]\label{thm:t1}
Let $\Lam\subset\Z$, $T$ \emph{any} subset of the rate-$Q$ grid $\{0,\dots,Q{-}1\}/Q$ with
$\sigma_{\min}(\Phimat)>0$ ($N\ge m$; $\Lam$ distinct mod $Q$), $\nu\in\Z$.
(i)~$\vt(\nu)=0$ iff $\bm\phi_\nu\in\range\Phimat$; if $\nu\equiv\omega_k\pmod Q$ then
$\bm\phi_\nu=\bm\phi_{\omega_k}$ \emph{on every such subset}, so $\vt(\nu)=0$, the fit is
the unit coefficient on atom $k$, and the training residual is exactly zero.
(ii)~With $f_1=f_{\mathrm{in}}+ae_\nu$, $f_2=f_{\mathrm{in}}+ae_{\omega_k}$
($\nu\equiv\omega_k$, $\nu\ne\omega_k$): $S_Tf_1=S_Tf_2$, so the observation laws coincide
under any noise and \emph{any} estimator has
$\max_i\norm{\hat f-f_i}_{L^2}\ge|a|/\sqrt2$.
(iii)~If $\nu\not\equiv\omega_k$ for all $k$ and $[\Phimat\;\bm\phi_\nu]$ has full column
rank then $\vt(\nu)>0$.
\end{theorem}
\emph{Sketch.}  (i)~$e^{i2\pi\nu g/Q}$ depends only on $\nu\bmod Q$; (ii)~two-point (Le
Cam) with $\norm{e_\nu-e_{\omega_k}}_{L^2}=\sqrt2$; (iii)~the rank condition (supplement).
\emph{Silent aliasing} is (i)--(ii): the samples are fit to the noise floor while the
reconstruction error is $\ge$ the full out-of-band energy, at any $N$, with $\nu$
arbitrarily far from $\Lam$.  The corrective is (i)'s \emph{on every such subset}: a
common intuition holds that irregular sampling avoids aliasing; on a grid it does not, no
matter how irregular the subset.  Classical aliasing is $T=$ full grid~\cite{shannon1949}.

\section{Randomization breaks it, at a rate}\label{sec:t3}
\begin{theorem}[Off-grid randomization]\label{thm:t3}
Let $\Lam\subset\Z$, $|\Lam|=m$.  (a)~\emph{(jitter)}  $t_j=g_j/Q+\eta_j$, $\eta_j$
i.i.d.\ mean-zero with characteristic function $\chi_\eta$, $\nu=\omega_k+rQ$
($r\ne0$).  For arbitrary $g_j$ the fold coherence $\frac1N\sum_je^{i2\pi(\nu-\omega_k)t_j}$
has mean $\chi_\eta(2\pi rQ)$ and concentrates at rate $2\sqrt{\log(4/\delta)/N}$; if the
$g_j$ are drawn i.i.d.\ uniformly and $\Lam$ is distinct mod $Q$ then
$\vt(\nu)^2=1-|\chi_\eta(2\pi rQ)|^2+O_P(m\sqrt{\log(m/\delta)/N})$, giving (Gaussian
$\sigma_t$) $\vt(\nu)\approx2\pi|r|Q\sigma_t$.
(b)~\emph{(i.i.d.)}  If the limiting normalized Gram is $\succeq\lambda_{\min}\bm I$ and
limiting model--candidate coherences vanish on a finite $\Om$, $|\Om|=K$
($\lambda_{\min}=1$ for the uniform density and integer-disjoint candidates), then with
$\varepsilon=2\sqrt{\log(4(mK+m^2)/\delta)/N}$, $m\varepsilon<\lambda_{\min}$, w.p.\
$\ge1-\delta$,
$\max_{\nu\in\Om}\at(\nu)\le\sqrt m\,\varepsilon/(\lambda_{\min}-m\varepsilon)$.
\end{theorem}
\emph{Sketch.}  (a)~the on-grid fold phase is $1$; jitter adds i.i.d.\ unit-modulus
factors (empirical characteristic function; Hoeffding).  (b)~Hoeffding$+$union over
$mK{+}m^2$ coherences, then a Neumann/perturbation bound on
$\at(\nu)=\norm{(\Phimat^{*}\Phimat/N)^{-1}(\Phimat^{*}\bm\phi_\nu/N)}$; the constants are
loose (a union bound) but the $N^{-1/2}$ rate is exact (Fig.~\ref{fig:matrix}a,b).  Two
honest boundaries: on-grid designs never escape Thm.~\ref{thm:t1}; and (b) controls only a
\emph{finite} $\Om$---the certificate of Sec.~\ref{sec:design} removes that limitation.

\section{Certified anti-aliasing sampling design}\label{sec:design}
Given $\Lam$, a candidate out-of-band set $\Om$ the practitioner wants visible, and a
budget $N$, we choose sample times to make $\Phimat$ well conditioned \emph{and}
$\Phimat^{*}\Psimat_\Om$ small ($\Psimat_\Om$ the $\Om$ atoms): jointly, high
$\min_\nu\vt(\nu)$ and low $\max_\nu\at(\nu)$ at small $\kappa(\Phimat)$.  This
asymmetric block target---protect $\Phimat^{*}\Phimat\!\to\!\bm I$, null
$\Phimat^{*}\Psimat_\Om\!\to\!0$, ignore the $\Om$ self-block---is precisely $D_s$-optimal
(nuisance-parameter) design~\cite{silvey1980optimal,atkinson2007optimum} and LCMV
null-steering~\cite{frost1972lcmv,vantrees2002optimum}; we credit those and add the
identifiability reading (which cross-terms, and why grid designs fail: Thm.~\ref{thm:t1})
and a certificate.

\emph{Surrogate and ablation.}  The exact objective is non-smooth and (via
$\Phimat^\dagger$) non-submodular---we claim \emph{no} greedy guarantee.  We minimize
$J(T)=\tfrac1{N^2}\sum_{k,\nu}|(\Phimat^{*}\Psimat_\Om)_{k\nu}|^2
+\beta\tfrac1{N^2}\sum_{k\ne l}|(\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)_{kl}|^2$, a function of the
difference-frequency sums, by coordinate descent over continuous times (fast rank-one
updates; deterministic given init; a grid-restricted greedy variant exists but inherits
Thm.~\ref{thm:t1} and cannot break exact folds).  Both terms are needed: on the near-band
regime of Fig.~\ref{fig:ag}a, coherence-only design suppresses aliasing
($\max\at\!=\!\AblCohA$) but at a worse condition number ($\kappa\!=\!\AblCohK$),
condition-only keeps $\kappa\!=\!\AblCondK$ but leaves $\max\at\!=\!\AblCondA$; only the
joint objective gets both ($\max\at\!=\!\AblAgA$ at $\kappa\!=\!\AblAgK$).

\emph{Continuum certificate.}  $\at(\nu)^2=\bm\phi_\nu^{*}\bm M\bm\phi_\nu$,
$\bm M=\Phimat(\Phimat^{*}\Phimat)^{-2}\Phimat^{*}$, is a real trigonometric polynomial in
$\nu$ with frequencies $t_l-t_j$; by a Bernstein/Lipschitz
bound~\cite{devore1993constructive} (its derivative is
$\le 2\pi\sum_{j,l}|\bm M_{jl}||t_l-t_j|$) a fine grid plus a slack term \emph{certifies}
$\max_{\nu\in[a,b]}\at(\nu)$ over an entire continuous band from the samples alone
(Prop.~\ref{prop:cert}; sound and tight in the tests).  This is exactly what
Thm.~\ref{thm:t3}(b) cannot give (finite $\Om$) and random designs have no analogue.

\begin{proposition}[Certificate]\label{prop:cert}
For any $T$, $\max_{\nu\in[a,b]}\at(\nu)\le\big(\max_{\text{grid}}\bm\phi^{*}\bm M\bm\phi
+L\,h/2\big)^{1/2}$ with grid spacing $h$ and $L=2\pi\sum_{j,l}|\bm M_{jl}||t_l-t_j|$; an
analogous bound lower-bounds $\min_{\nu\in[a,b]}\vt(\nu)$.
\end{proposition}

\emph{Result.}  Designed on a focused, thickened concern set and evaluated on
\emph{held-out} perturbed frequencies it never saw, the design cuts worst-case
aliasability to $\AGheld{\pm}\AGheldCI$ vs.\ $\AGrand$ (random jitter) and $\AGeopt$
(E-optimal), and stays at $\AGmis$ under candidate-set misspecification
(Fig.~\ref{fig:ag}b); the advantage persists across the budget sweep (Fig.~\ref{fig:ag}c)
and translates to lower signal-domain aliasing bias at equal noise gain
(Fig.~\ref{fig:ag}d).  Over a continuous band it \emph{certifies}
$\max\at\le\AGcert$ vs.\ $\AGcertR$ for random.  It generalizes to 2-D frequency vectors
($\max\at$ $\AGtwoD$ vs.\ $\AGtwoDR$).  Honest scope: the tool applies where sample
\emph{times are chosen}---active sampling, measurement/acquisition design, or scheduling
a coordinate network's training queries---not to already-acquired data; it needs a
focused concern set ($K\!=\!O(N)$), and for a broad band densely covered with $K\!\gg\!N$
no design beats random much, while a badly misspecified set loses the edge.

\section{Experiments}\label{sec:exp}
All closed forms are Monte-Carlo validated (39 tests; CI on every push).  JSONs carry
seeds, configs, and environment stamps; every figure is regenerated from them.

\emph{Theory (Fig.~\ref{fig:matrix}).}  (a)~the jitter law matches $\sqrt{1-\chi_\eta^2}$
across two decades, exact fold ($v\!\approx\!10^{-14}$) at $\sigma_t{=}0$; (b)~i.i.d.\
worst-case aliasability decays $N^{-1/2}$ beneath the bound; (c)~the grid-subset coherent
tone stays invisible at every $N$ while i.i.d.\ sampling makes even the per-$N$ adversarial
candidate visible; (d)~jitter removes the aliasing-bias term, never truncation.

\emph{Design (Fig.~\ref{fig:ag}).}  The four panels above; the joint objective, held-out
generalization, budget sweep, and signal-domain payoff.

\emph{Real signals, sample-only.}  On CO$_2$, sunspots, and nine speech segments
(anti-aliased decimation; full records), sample-only Lomb--Scargle bandwidth selection
attains RMSE $\NumCoTwoLS$ vs.\ an evaluation-only oracle $\NumCoTwoOracle$ on CO$_2$;
on broadband speech \emph{no} selector beats RMSE${\approx}\NumSpeechOracle$---the
truncation term dominates at that budget (the real-data face of the converse).  Details,
the detection dichotomy (a coherent fold is indistinguishable from its in-band twin by any
sample-only test; visible tones follow an exact noncentral-$\chi^2$ power curve matched by
calibrated detectors), and a trained-network extension (FF-MLP fold match $77\%$/corr
$0.96$ over $\ge20$ seeds; \emph{SIREN fails}, corr $0.49$---trained SIRENs leave their
init NTK, so the theory makes no claim there) with 2-D results are in the supplement, with
all failing runs released.

\section{Limitations and conclusion}
The theory covers fixed-feature linear-coefficient models; nonlinear networks are an
empirical extension that \emph{fails for SIRENs}, and adaptive frequencies are open.  The
concentration constants are loose (union bound); the design needs a focused concern set.
Within that scope: irregular-on-a-grid sampling does not break exact aliasing
(Thm.~\ref{thm:t1}); off-grid randomization does, at an explicit finite-$N$ rate
(Thm.~\ref{thm:t3}); and a $D_s$-optimal / null-steering design with a continuum
certificate turns the analysis into a deployable, guaranteed anti-aliasing sample pattern.
Practical guidance: never sample purely on a grid when out-of-band content is possible;
target a focused concern set; and certify the band, not a finite list.
